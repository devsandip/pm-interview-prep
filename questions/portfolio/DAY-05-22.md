## Question 1

**Question:**  
You're the new CPO at Substack — what's your 100-day plan to unlock the next phase of growth?

**Company Context:**

**Business:** Substack is a publishing platform where writers run paid newsletters and grow direct, subscription-based relationships with readers. Recently expanded into video, podcasts, Notes (Twitter-like short-form), Chat, and a recommendation network. Two-sided marketplace: writers create, readers subscribe.

**Current State:**
- Revenue: ~$45M ARR (estimated 2025), grows ~30% YoY
- GMV (subscription dollars flowing through): ~$300M annually, ~$45M+ to top creators
- Take rate: 10% on paid subscriptions
- Scale: ~5M paid subscriptions, ~50M total subscribers (free + paid), ~2M active publications
- Stage: Series B, ~$90M raised, last valued at $650M (down round in 2023 from $650M ATH)
- Margins: Mixed — payments + Stripe fees eat ~3%, infra cheap, but heavy spend on Notes/video infra and creator deals

**The Constraint:**
Growth has hit a ceiling shaped like a power law. The top 250 newsletters generate the majority of Substack's revenue, but new writer acquisition is mostly cannibalizing existing readers (subscription budget is finite — readers churn one to subscribe to another). Net new paid subscriptions per month have plateaued for 4+ quarters.
- Free-to-paid conversion: ~6% (industry good, but flat for 18 months)
- Top 10 publications drive ~25% of GMV — extreme concentration risk
- ~70% of paid subscribers subscribe to only 1 publication; bundle/multi-pub adoption stuck below 10%
- Notes (the Twitter-clone) drove engagement but did NOT meaningfully drive paid conversion — most-engaged Notes users are existing power readers, not net new
- Video creators on Substack growing fast but earn ~1/10th what YouTube/Patreon pays for comparable audience

**Recent Context:**
- Twitter/X relationship hostile — Substack links throttled since 2023, blocking what used to be the #1 referral source
- Beehiiv (free + ad-supported model) crossed 25K active publications and is poaching mid-tier writers with better growth tools
- Patreon launched native video + community, going head-to-head for the creator-to-fan subscription category
- Ghost and Kit (formerly ConvertKit) winning the "self-host / own your data" indie writer
- CEO publicly resisted ads on Substack, but board reportedly pushing for new revenue streams
- AI-generated newsletters becoming a quality/spam problem

**Resources Available:**
- ~140 employees, ~50 in product+eng
- Cash runway: ~18 months at current burn
- 100 days to define and ship the first 1-2 visible bets; 12-month horizon to prove a new growth loop
- Mandate from board: re-accelerate paid subscriptions growth AND identify a credible second revenue line

## Question 2

**Question:**  
What would you build if you were CEO of Urban Company? The board wants the next leg of growth — where do you take the company over the next 3 years?

**Company Context:**

**Business:** Urban Company (formerly UrbanClap) is India's largest at-home services marketplace — salon, cleaning, appliance repair, plumbing, electrical, AC servicing, painting, etc. Connects vetted, trained service professionals ("partners") with consumers. Operates in India, UAE, Saudi Arabia, and Singapore. Sells its own branded products (Native water purifier, smart locks) through the partner network.

**Current State:**
- Revenue: ~₹1,150 Cr (~$140M) in FY25, grew ~35% YoY
- Bookings/GMV: ~₹2,800 Cr; ~10M annual transacting customers
- Take rate: ~25-30% (high — partners often complain it should be lower)
- Partners: ~50K active service professionals globally; ~30K in India
- Stage: filed DRHP for IPO in early 2025; last private valuation ~$2.8B (2021)
- Unit economics: India business EBITDA-positive in FY25 for the first time; international still loss-making but improving
- Native (own products): contributes ~15% of revenue, growing 60%+ YoY

**The Constraint:**
Growth is hitting structural limits on three sides simultaneously:
- **Supply**: Recruiting and retaining quality partners is the hard part. ~40% of recruited partners drop off within 6 months. Frequent partner protests over commission rates, lead allocation, and rating systems generate PR risk.
- **Demand**: Penetration is deep among urban affluent SEC A households in metros, but breaking into tier 2 cities is hard — willingness to pay for "premium home services" is much lower outside the top 8 cities. Repeat frequency stuck at ~3.5 transactions/year per customer.
- **Adjacency**: Native (own products) is the highest-margin growth engine, but the Native team and the services team have different DNA, GTM, and capital needs — there's debate about whether to spin Native out or double down.

**Recent Context:**
- IPO filing creates pressure for a clean, focused growth story in the next 6-12 months
- Apna Mart, Pronto, Snabbit, and other quick-service / quick-home-services startups attacking lower price points with 30-min response times in specific verticals
- Saudi Arabia revenue grew 90%+ YoY in FY25 — international clearly working, but capital-intensive
- ESOP buyback in late 2025 burned some cash; war chest is meaningful but not unlimited
- Hyperlocal AI assistants (WhatsApp-based service booking) emerging as a disintermediation risk

**Resources Available:**
- ~3,500 employees globally
- ~₹2,000 Cr cash + IPO proceeds (estimated ₹4,000-5,000 Cr if IPO clears)
- 3-year time horizon to prove a story worthy of a $5B+ post-IPO valuation
- Strong brand equity in metro India
