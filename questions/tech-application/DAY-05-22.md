## Question 1

**Question:**  
How would you use LLMs (and agentic LLM workflows) at Duolingo to accelerate user growth — specifically to lift activation, retention, and net-new MAU?

**Domain/Company Context:**

**What they do:**  
Duolingo is the world's largest language learning app — gamified, freemium, mobile-first. Free tier supports ads; Super Duolingo (~$7/mo) and Duolingo Max (~$30/mo, includes GPT-4 "Roleplay" + "Explain My Answer") are paid tiers. Courses span 40+ languages plus Math, Music, and Chess.

**Current tech:**
- Mature data + ML platform; long-running "Birdbrain" SRS personalization model
- GPT-4 integration for Duolingo Max (Roleplay conversation partner, Explain My Answer feedback)
- In-house content generation pipeline (Duolingo replaced contractors with LLM-assisted content authoring in 2024)
- A/B testing infrastructure ("CourseRunner") used aggressively (hundreds of experiments/quarter)
- Mature mobile-first stack (React Native + native components)

**Pain points:**
- **Activation cliff**: ~50% of new sign-ups never return after Day 1; <20% reach Day 7
- **Course gaps**: Long-tail languages (Vietnamese, Tamil, Tagalog) and underserved domains (Music, Chess) have shallow content trees → low retention for those learners
- **Speaking practice is the #1 churn-reducer**, but lessons are still mostly tap-to-translate. Real speaking practice is gated behind Duolingo Max (~$30/mo) → poor reach
- **Cohort plateau in mature markets** (US, UK, India urban) — incremental MAU growth depends on lower CAC channels or new user segments (kids, professionals, immigrants)
- **Content velocity vs. quality**: LLM-authored content is cheaper but sometimes hallucinates idioms / grammar edge cases, particularly in non-Indo-European languages

**Scale:**
- ~115M MAU, ~38M DAU
- ~10M paid subscribers (Super + Max)
- ~1.5B lessons completed/month
- ~1,200 employees; ~600 in product+eng+data
- 40+ languages; ~100 courses

**Constraints:**
- **Data**: Massive interaction telemetry (every tap, every answer, every streak event) — best in industry. Speech data is thinner — only Max users practice speaking at meaningful volume.
- **Technical maturity**: LLMs proven for content + tutoring; agentic LLMs (planning, tool use, multi-turn) still emerging. Real-time speech (latency <500ms) requires on-device or edge models — Apple Intelligence + Gemini Nano now make this feasible on flagship phones, but the majority of users in India/LatAm/Africa are on mid-range Android.
- **Cost**: Free-tier ARPU is ~$1-2/year — adding LLM inference to free tier blows up unit economics unless inference is dirt cheap or distilled to small models. Max ARPU (~$360/yr) easily absorbs LLM costs.
- **Regulatory**: COPPA / DPDP for under-13 users (Duolingo Math is popular with kids); regional content compliance (e.g., political/historical references in Chinese, Russian courses)
- **Brand**: Duolingo's brand is built on whimsy and learning science — buggy AI hurts more than for a chatbot product

## Question 2

**Question:**  
How would you use computer vision at Meesho to drive growth of new buyers and reduce returns/cancellations from existing buyers?

**Domain/Company Context:**

**What they do:**  
Meesho is India's largest value-conscious e-commerce marketplace, focused on tier 2/3 cities and price-sensitive shoppers. ~1.5M sellers (mostly small/unbranded), zero seller commission, makes money on logistics + ads. Catalog skewed to apparel, home, beauty, kitchen.

**Current tech:**
- Standard e-commerce stack (search, recommendations, checkout)
- Text-based search dominates; visual search shipped in 2024 but limited adoption
- Catalog moderation is partly manual (3,000+ ops people), partly rule-based
- Lightweight personalization (collaborative filtering + simple two-tower retrieval)
- Hindi/regional language voice search added in 2025

**Pain points:**
- **Search abandonment**: ~35% of buyer search sessions end with no purchase — high rate of "I can't find what I'm looking for" especially for non-English speakers describing fashion
- **Catalog quality**: Sellers upload poor-quality images, duplicate listings, mislabeled categories → discovery suffers; ~12% of listings flagged for quality issues
- **Returns/cancellations**: Apparel return rate ~25-30%; "color/fit not as shown" is the #1 stated reason. RTO (return-to-origin) costs Meesho ~₹70-90 per failed delivery; ~18% of orders cancel before delivery
- **New buyer activation**: Tier 3/4 cities have lower English literacy; text-based search is a friction wall for first-time online shoppers
- **Counterfeit + duplicate listings**: Branded knockoffs and same-image-different-seller listings hurt trust and dilute search

**Scale:**
- ~175M annual transacting users, ~16M MAU
- ~10M monthly orders
- ~120M live SKUs on catalog
- ~1.5M sellers; ~3,000 ops moderators
- Operates pan-India, 99% of pincodes serviced

**Constraints:**
- **Data**: Massive image catalog (~120M product images, ~50M user-uploaded review images) — best-in-class asset. But labeled data for Indian apparel categories (saree, kurta, lehenga drape styles) is thin compared to Western categories.
- **Technical maturity**: Visual search + visual similarity is mature (Pinterest, Amazon ship it). Vision-language models (CLIP, Gemini Vision) are increasingly cheap to run at retrieval time. Try-on for apparel is harder for Indian body types and ethnic wear — model quality lower than for Western fashion.
- **Cost**: ARPU is low (~₹150/user/year); any per-query CV cost must stay sub-cent. Indexing 120M images with embeddings is a meaningful infra cost (~$1-2M/year) but tractable.
- **Device**: ~60% of Meesho buyers are on entry-level Android phones with limited camera quality and weak on-device compute → server-side inference required for most CV.
- **Trust**: False-positive flags on small sellers (suspected counterfeit, wrong category) cause seller churn — moderation precision matters as much as recall.
