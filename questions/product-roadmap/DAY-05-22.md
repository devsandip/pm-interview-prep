## Question 1

**Question:**  
Build a 3-year strategy to grow Figma's weekly active users (WAU) from 8M to 25M by expanding beyond designers into non-design roles (PMs, engineers, marketers, sales).

**Context:**  
Figma is the browser-based collaborative design platform, now also home to FigJam (whiteboarding), Dev Mode (engineering handoff), Slides (presentations), and Sites (no-code publishing).

**Current state:**
- Revenue: ~$1.1B ARR (2025), growing ~40% YoY post-Adobe deal collapse
- Users: 8M WAU; ~4M are core designers, ~4M are "viewers/commenters" (PMs, devs, leadership)
- Penetration: Strong in design teams (95%+ of design-led companies) but only ~15% of seats in those companies are paid editors
- Current model: Per-editor seat pricing ($15 Professional, $45 Organization), free for viewers
- Conversion: Most non-designers stay as free viewers; ~10% upgrade to paid editor seats annually

**The constraint:**
Figma has saturated the designer market — there are only so many designers in the world (~5M globally). To grow WAU 3x, Figma needs adjacent personas to *create*, not just view. But non-designers find the core canvas intimidating, and FigJam/Slides/Sites haven't crossed into mainstream non-designer workflows. Meanwhile, Canva is growing the bottom-up "everyone is a designer" wedge with 220M MAU, and Microsoft is bundling Loop + Designer into Microsoft 365 at zero marginal cost.

**Competitive landscape:**
- **Canva**: 220M MAU, $3B ARR, dominant in "non-designer creates a thing" use cases (social, decks, docs); recently acquired Affinity to attack pro designers
- **Microsoft Loop + Designer + Whiteboard**: Bundled in M365, free for 400M+ paid seats, weak UX but zero adoption friction
- **Miro**: 80M users, owns the whiteboard category for non-designers
- **Notion**: 100M+ users, encroaching from the docs side with embedded design surfaces

**Strategic opportunity:**
Multiplayer collaboration is Figma's structural moat — Canva and Microsoft tools still feel single-player. If Figma can build "Figma for [PMs/engineers/marketers]" — opinionated surfaces that wrap the canvas with familiar workflows (PM specs, eng tickets, campaign briefs) — it can convert the 50M+ knowledge workers already viewing Figma files into paid editors. AI dramatically lowers the "I can't design" barrier: generative templates, natural-language layouts, and intelligent suggestions can make a non-designer productive in minutes. Win this, and Figma stops being "design tool" and starts being "collaborative work surface" — a 10x larger TAM.

## Question 2

**Question:**  
Build a 3-year strategy to grow PhonePe's monthly transacting users (MTU) from 350M to 550M while reducing dependence on UPI peer-to-peer transfers (which generate zero revenue).

**Context:**  
PhonePe is India's largest UPI payments app and a growing financial services platform offering insurance, mutual funds, lending, gold, and merchant services.

**Current state:**
- Revenue: ~₹5,000 Cr (~$600M) in FY25, ~74% YoY growth, still loss-making (~₹1,000 Cr loss)
- Users: 350M MTU; 600M registered; ~48% UPI market share (vs Google Pay's ~37%, Paytm declining)
- Transaction mix: ~92% of transactions are P2P / P2M UPI (zero MDR, zero revenue); only ~8% are revenue-generating (insurance, lending, merchant services, ads, bill payments)
- ARPU: ~₹170/year per MTU (vs Paytm's ~₹350, Kotak811's ~₹1,500)
- Distribution: 40M+ merchants accepting PhonePe QR; deep in tier 2/3 cities

**The constraint:**
PhonePe's growth is structurally bottlenecked. NPCI's 30% UPI market share cap (originally 2024, repeatedly deferred but still looming) limits raw transaction growth. Worse, UPI itself is a free utility — every new P2P user *costs* PhonePe money to acquire and serve with zero revenue per transaction. To grow MTU 60% AND turn profitable, PhonePe needs new users coming in through *value-added* hooks (lending, insurance, commerce) rather than just UPI, and existing users converting from "free payments" into "financial services" customers. Meanwhile, jio finance, navi, and CRED are aggressively poaching the high-ARPU urban user.

**Competitive landscape:**
- **Google Pay**: 37% UPI share, deep integration with Android, weak on financial services — vulnerable
- **Paytm**: Hammered by RBI restrictions on Payments Bank; market share collapsing from 14% to ~7%; opportunity to absorb their merchants
- **CRED**: 15M premium urban users, ~₹3,000+ ARPU, owns the credit-card/affluent wedge
- **Jio Finance**: Bundled free with Jio mobile, AJio commerce, MyJio super-app; existential long-term threat in tier 2/3
- **Navi / Slice / Fi**: Neobanks pulling young urban users into full-stack banking

**Strategic opportunity:**
PhonePe has India's largest distribution moat in financial services — 350M users, 40M merchants, and trust in tier 2/3 cities that no neobank has. The Indian financial services market is shifting from "transaction" to "credit + investment": MF AUM doubling to ₹100L Cr by 2028, retail lending growing 18% CAGR, insurance penetration still under 5%. If PhonePe converts even 5% of its base into lending/insurance customers, that's a ~₹15,000 Cr revenue opportunity. The 2026 IPO timeline (filed DRHP late 2025) makes the next 36 months critical for proving a non-UPI revenue thesis. Account Aggregator framework, DPI rails, and ONDC unlock new commerce + credit flows that didn't exist 18 months ago.
