## Question 1 (MILEJPSR)

**Question:**  
How would you improve YouTube Shorts to grow Shorts watch-time and net-new YouTube creators (not just cannibalize long-form viewing)?

**Product Context:**
- **What it does:** YouTube Shorts is YouTube's vertical short-form video product (60-second-or-less videos), competing directly with TikTok and Instagram Reels.
- **Current state:** ~70B daily Shorts views, ~2B monthly logged-in viewers, available in 100+ countries. Shorts is monetized through the YouTube Partner Program (revenue share from a Creator Pool funded by ads + YouTube Premium), but per-view payouts are still ~10-30% of long-form CPMs.
- **Known challenges:**
  - Cannibalization risk: a large share of Shorts watch-time appears to come from existing long-form viewers, not net new audience or net new creators
  - Creator monetization on Shorts is significantly weaker than long-form, so top creators still optimize for the 8-15 min format
  - Discovery handoff from Shorts → long-form / Subscribe is weak; viewers consume hundreds of Shorts without ever subscribing to the creator
  - TikTok ban dynamics and Reels' growth keep competitive pressure high

**Time Limit:** 25 minutes

**Recommended Framework:** MILEJPSR  
(This is an "improve [Product]" question with rich strategic context — Mission, Insights, Levers, Experiments, Judgment, Prioritization, Scaling, Risks all apply.)

**Framework Location:** `plugins/product-sense/framework-milejpsr.md`

**Evaluation Criteria:**
- Did you clarify the goal? (Shorts watch-time vs. net-new creators vs. cross-pollination to long-form — these can conflict)
- Did you segment users and pick ONE primary persona to design for (e.g., aspiring creator who has TikTok but not YouTube)?
- Did you map the current Shorts experience and identify a real, non-obvious pain (not "discovery is bad")?
- Did you generate 3+ diverse solutions and force trade-offs?
- Did you choose a north-star metric and counter-metric (e.g., Shorts-driven Subscribes vs. long-form watch-time per Shorts viewer)?
- Did you show conviction in the recommendation and acknowledge cannibalization risk explicitly?

## Question 2 (CIRCLES)

**Question:**  
Design a product to help international students moving to a new country for the first time settle into their first 30 days.

**Context:**  
Assume you're designing this for a major tech company (your choice — Google, Meta, Microsoft, or a fintech like Wise) as a growth bet into a high-LTV segment. International students are roughly 6M+ globally and growing ~8% YoY post-pandemic. They face a compressed, high-friction window: housing, bank account, SIM, transport, university paperwork, social connection — all in 2-4 weeks. They're young, mobile-native, often credit-thin, and highly word-of-mouth driven. Success = repeat use during the first 30 days, organic referrals to the next cohort, and conversion to a longer-term financial / community / utility relationship.

**Time Limit:** 20 minutes

**Recommended Framework:** CIRCLES  
(Open-ended "design a product for [use case]" — CIRCLES is well-suited to the heavy clarification this needs: which country corridors? On-arrival vs. pre-departure? Free vs. paid? Distribution channel?)

**Framework Location:** `plugins/product-sense/framework-circles.md`

**Evaluation Criteria:**
- **C — Clarify:** Did you ask about corridor (India → US? China → UK? Africa → Canada?), launching company, success metric, free vs. paid, and time horizon?
- **I — Identify customer:** Did you segment international students (grad vs. undergrad, well-funded vs. self-funded, English-fluent vs. not) and pick ONE?
- **R — Report needs:** Did you list real needs (housing trust, banking onboarding, peer connection, parental reassurance) — not features?
- **C — Cut (prioritize) needs:** Did you pick ONE need to anchor on and defend why?
- **L — List solutions:** Did you generate 3+ meaningfully different solutions (e.g., a banking-first wedge vs. a community-first wedge vs. a paperwork-concierge wedge)?
- **E — Evaluate trade-offs:** Did you compare on impact, feasibility, and growth-loop strength?
- **S — Summarize:** Did you pick one with conviction, propose a metric, and call out the biggest risk?
