## Question 1

**Question:**  
What's the difference between Product-Market Fit (PMF) and Product-Channel Fit (PCF)?

**Follow-up:**  
Give a real-world example of a product that has PMF but struggles with PCF — and one that has both.

**Time Limit:** 5 minutes

**Recommended Structure:**
1. **Define each term** (60 sec)
   - PMF: The product solves a real problem for a clearly defined market, evidenced by retention, repeat use, and organic pull
   - PCF: There is a *repeatable, scalable, cost-effective channel* that consistently delivers the right users into the product
2. **State the key distinction** (60 sec) — PMF is about whether users *love* and *stay*; PCF is about whether you can *reach* them economically. PMF without PCF = a great product nobody hears about (or that you can only acquire via paid that doesn't pencil out).
3. **Give an example** (90 sec)
   - PMF without PCF: Many vertical B2B SaaS tools (e.g., niche dental practice software) — high retention among users, but the only channel that works is expensive outbound sales, capping growth velocity
   - PMF + PCF together: Notion (PMF via templates/flexibility + PCF via viral team adoption and SEO-rich templates), or in India: CRED (PMF for affluent card users + PCF via referral + performance marketing tuned to a narrow audience)
4. **When to use each lens** (60 sec) — Use PMF lens during 0→1 (am I solving a real problem?); use PCF lens during 1→10 (how do I grow without CAC blowing up?). A startup that thinks it has PMF but can't grow is almost always missing PCF, not PMF.

## Question 2

**Question:**  
What's the difference between a viral loop and a growth loop?

**Follow-up:**  
Give an example of each, and explain when a growth team should prioritize building one vs. the other.

**Time Limit:** 5 minutes

**Recommended Structure:**
1. **Define each term** (60 sec)
   - Viral loop: A specific type of loop where *existing users bring in new users* through invitations, shares, or referrals; output of one user's action is direct exposure to another user
   - Growth loop: A self-reinforcing system where the *output of one cycle becomes the input to the next* — can be powered by users, content, paid acquisition, data, or any combination. Viral loops are *one species* of growth loop.
2. **State the key distinction** (60 sec) — All viral loops are growth loops; not all growth loops are viral. Growth loops can be powered by SEO content, UGC, paid-LTV reinvestment, marketplace liquidity, etc. — none of which require user-to-user invites.
3. **Give an example** (90 sec)
   - Viral loop: WhatsApp (you join because a friend invites you; the more you message, the more friends you pull on). Dropbox referral program (give space, get space).
   - Non-viral growth loop: Pinterest's SEO loop (users create pins → pins rank in Google → new visitors land on Pinterest → some sign up → create more pins). Or Uber's paid-LTV loop (acquire rider via paid → rider's LTV funds acquiring 2 more riders).
4. **When to prioritize each** (60 sec)
   - Viral loops work when the product *gets better with more people you know* (messengers, social, collaboration tools) — prioritize these for B2C network products
   - Non-viral growth loops are essential when product utility is individual (Pinterest, Spotify, a marketplace) — viral is weak, so you need content/SEO/paid/data loops to compound. Building only for virality on a single-player product is a common failure mode.
