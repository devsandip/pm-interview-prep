## Question 1

**Question:**  
Should Zomato Gold prioritize **deepening benefits for existing members (depth)** or **lowering the price/eligibility bar to acquire many more new members (breadth)** over the next 12 months?

**The Trade-off:**

**Option A: Deepen benefits for existing members (Depth)**
- What it is: Keep Gold pricing at ~₹399/quarter (~₹1,500/yr equivalent), but materially expand what Gold unlocks — bigger "Free Delivery" radius, free delivery on Blinkit (Zomato's quick commerce), guaranteed-on-time refunds, exclusive restaurant slots/reservations, partner perks (BookMyShow, OTT bundles)
- Key benefit: Higher per-member LTV, better retention (renewal % moves up), stronger moat vs. Swiggy One; reinforces Gold as a *premium identity*, not just a coupon
- Key cost: Slower top-of-funnel — Gold member count grows incrementally, not exponentially. Higher cost-per-benefit eats into already-thin food delivery margins. Risk that existing members renew but the program doesn't reach a meaningfully larger base.

**Option B: Lower price + broaden eligibility (Breadth)**
- What it is: Cut entry-tier Gold to ~₹99/month or ~₹799/year, push aggressive intro offers (1 month free, bundle with first food order), and possibly bundle Gold into telco/credit-card partnerships to push the member count toward 25M+ in 12 months
- Key benefit: Much bigger member base → more orders per cohort, more frequency, more attach to Blinkit, more pricing power with restaurants ("you're missing 25M Gold buyers"), defensive against Swiggy One Lite
- Key cost: Average benefits-per-member must shrink (or margins crater); Gold loses some "premium" cachet; cannibalization of existing full-price subscribers; profitability of the food delivery segment (which Zomato has worked years to defend) takes a near-term hit

**Why they're in tension:**
Subscription growth has two dials — *willingness to pay* and *willingness to enroll*. Going deep raises ARPU per member but slows enrollment; going broad accelerates enrollment but compresses ARPU and per-member economics. With Swiggy One/One Lite undercutting on price and Blinkit (now owned by Eternal/Zomato parent) needing subscription gravity of its own, Zomato has to pick which dial dominates the 12-month plan.

**Product Context:**
Zomato Gold is the loyalty/subscription program of Zomato (now under the Eternal Ltd umbrella). Roughly 8-10M paying Gold members; food delivery handles ~3M orders/day with Gold members responsible for an outsized share (estimated 35-45% of orders, much higher AOV). India's food delivery market is a Zomato-Swiggy duopoly; both are EBITDA-positive in food delivery, both burning in quick commerce (Blinkit vs Instamart).

Current challenge: Gold member growth has decelerated from ~80% YoY to ~25% YoY over the past four quarters even as overall orders grow. Swiggy One Lite (₹99/month) launched in early 2026 and is bleeding off prospective Gold buyers at the lower price point.

**Business Context:**
- **Mission:** Better food for more people (Zomato/Eternal level)
- **Strategic priority:** Defend food delivery profitability while using subscription to *anchor* the Blinkit + Hyperpure + District (going-out) ecosystem; sub gravity is becoming the moat
- **Competitive landscape:** Swiggy One + One Lite is the direct competitor; Amazon Prime (in India) is the secondary threat as a "super-subscription"; ONDC keeps independent-restaurant defection a tail risk
- **Product phase:** Mature in food delivery, scaling in quick commerce/District; subscription is mid-growth and at a strategic inflection

**Additional Context:**
- Internal data: Gold members' renewal rate is ~75% at the existing price; first-year-trial-to-paid conversion is ~40%
- Gold members order 3-4x more frequently than non-members; AOV is ~25% higher
- Blinkit attach: ~55% of Gold food-delivery members have also placed at least one Blinkit order; Gold-style benefits on Blinkit could meaningfully lift Blinkit frequency
- CFO is publicly committed to food delivery EBITDA margin expansion year-over-year
- Engineering capacity: any "depth" benefits like partner bundles, free Blinkit delivery, reservations require meaningful cross-team coordination (Blinkit + restaurants + payments) — 6-9 month build, not a quarter
