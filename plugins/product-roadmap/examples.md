# Product Roadmap Example Questions

## Example 1: Monetization Focus

**Question:**  
Build a 3-year monetization strategy for Spotify Podcasts

**Context:**  
Spotify Podcasts is the audio streaming platform's entry into on-demand talk content.

**Current state:**
- Revenue: Podcasts drive <5% of Spotify's $13B revenue
- Users: 500M total Spotify users, 25% listen to podcasts weekly
- Growth: Podcast listening up 40% YoY, but monetization lagging
- Current model: Host-read ads + dynamic ad insertion only

**The constraint:**
Podcast monetization severely underdeveloped compared to music. YouTube and Apple Podcasts launched creator monetization (subscriptions, tipping, premium content) in 2024-25, putting pressure on Spotify to offer similar tools or risk losing top creators.

**Competitive landscape:**
- YouTube Podcasts: Video + audio, strong creator monetization (Super Thanks, memberships), eating into Spotify's video podcast share
- Apple Podcasts: Native subscriptions, lower commission (15%) than Spotify could charge

**Strategic opportunity:**
Spotify has exclusive deals with major podcasters (Joe Rogan, Call Her Daddy) expiring 2026-27. Need to build creator monetization tools to re-sign them without paying $100M+ guarantees. Subscriptions + tipping could generate $2B+ by Year 3.

## Example 2: Growth Focus

**Question:**  
Build a 3-year strategy to grow LinkedIn Learning MAU from 20M to 100M

**Context:**  
LinkedIn Learning is the professional development platform bundled with LinkedIn Premium.

**Current state:**
- Revenue: ~$800M (part of $15B LinkedIn total)
- Users: 20M Learning users (vs 950M total LinkedIn users - 2% penetration)
- Growth: Stagnant at 20M for 2 years
- Current model: Bundled with Premium ($40/month), standalone rarely purchased

**The constraint:**
LinkedIn Learning is a "nice to have" add-on, not core to LinkedIn's value prop. Most users don't realize they have access (if Premium) or see the relevance (courses feel corporate/boring). Meanwhile, YouTube has 10x the learning content for free, and Udemy has 60M users.

**Competitive landscape:**
- YouTube: Free, massive scale, better discovery (algorithm-driven)
- Udemy: Pay-per-course, 60M users, perceived as more practical
- Coursera: University partnerships, 130M users, stronger brand for "serious" learning

**Strategic opportunity:**
LinkedIn has the professional graph (know your job, skills, career goals) and job postings (know what skills employers want). Can create a personalized, career-focused learning experience competitors can't match. "Learn the skills for the job you want" is a unique wedge.

**Time Limit:** 25 minutes

**Recommended Framework:** MATRIX Framework

**Framework Location:** `/plugins/product-roadmap/framework.md`

## Example 3: Competitive Focus

**Question:**  
Build a 3-year roadmap for Notion to compete with Microsoft 365

**Context:**  
Notion is a productivity/collaboration tool competing with Microsoft, Google, and Atlassian.

**Current state:**
- Revenue: ~$500M ARR (estimated)
- Users: 30M users, 100K+ teams
- Growth: 100%+ YoY for 3 years, now slowing to 50% YoY
- Current model: Freemium ($10/user/month for teams)

**The constraint:**
Microsoft is bundling Copilot (AI) into Office 365 at scale, making it harder for Notion to compete on AI alone. Large enterprises hesitate to adopt Notion because it lacks: email, calendar, advanced permissions, compliance certifications (SOC 2 Type II, HIPAA).

**Competitive landscape:**
- Microsoft 365: 345M paid seats, enterprise grip, now has AI (Copilot) built-in
- Google Workspace: 3B users, strong in SMB/education, also adding AI (Duet)
- Atlassian: Strong in engineering teams (Jira, Confluence), weak in general knowledge work

**Strategic opportunity:**
Notion has the most flexible, customizable workspace (databases, views, templates). Microsoft/Google are legacy tools rebuilt with AI on top. Notion can be "workspace OS" - where you do ALL work (docs, projects, wikis, AND email/calendar) with AI deeply integrated, not bolted on.

**Time Limit:** 25 minutes

**Recommended Framework:** MATRIX Framework

**Framework Location:** `/plugins/product-roadmap/framework.md`

## Example 4: Indian Market Focus

**Question:**  
Build a 3-year monetization roadmap for Swiggy Instamart (quick commerce)

**Context:**  
Swiggy Instamart is the 10-minute grocery delivery service competing with Zepto, Blinkit, and BigBasket.

**Current state:**
- GMV: $1.2B annually
- Orders: 50M orders/month across 25 cities
- Unit economics: Losing ₹40/order (negative margins)
- Current model: Free delivery above ₹99, earns commission (5-15%) from brands

**The constraint:**
Quick commerce is a race to the bottom on pricing. Competitors (Zepto, Blinkit) are subsidizing deliveries to gain market share. Swiggy's food delivery is profitable, but Instamart is burning $200M/year. Investors want path to profitability.

**Competitive landscape:**
- Zepto: $2B valuation, raised $1B recently, strongest in Mumbai/Bangalore, dark stores optimized for speed
- Blinkit (Zomato): Integrated with Zomato food delivery, 600+ dark stores, also unprofitable
- BigBasket: Traditional e-grocery (not 10-min), profitable, but losing market share to quick commerce

**Strategic opportunity:**
Swiggy has 8M Swiggy One subscribers (₹149/month for free delivery on food + Instamart). Can bundle premium features, increase basket size through personalization, and leverage food delivery network (shared dark stores reduce costs).

**Time Limit:** 25 minutes

**Recommended Framework:** MATRIX Framework

**Framework Location:** `/plugins/product-roadmap/framework.md`

## Example 5: Platform Evolution

**Question:**  
Build a 3-year strategy for Instagram to evolve from social network to creator economy platform

**Context:**  
Instagram is shifting from friend-to-friend social to creator-to-audience content platform.

**Current state:**
- Revenue: $50B (part of Meta's $134B total)
- Users: 2B users, 500M creators
- Monetization: Ads primarily, creator monetization nascent (badges, subscriptions just launched)
- Current model: Creators earn through brand deals (outside Instagram), Instagram earns through ads

**The constraint:**
TikTok and YouTube have better creator monetization (Creator Fund, tipping, subscriptions, revenue share). Instagram creators complain they do the work, Instagram/Meta takes the ad revenue. Risk of losing top creators to platforms that pay better.

**Competitive landscape:**
- TikTok: $14B Creator Fund, growing fast, better organic reach
- YouTube: Ad revenue share (55% to creators), Super Thanks, memberships, $16B paid to creators in 2023
- Patreon: Direct creator-to-fan payment, $300M+ to creators annually

**Strategic opportunity:**
Instagram has the audience (2B users) and creator base (500M). Can build native monetization tools (subscriptions, tipping, revenue share on Reels ads) to capture the $104B creator economy market and keep creators on platform instead of sending them to Patreon/YouTube.

**Time Limit:** 25 minutes

**Recommended Framework:** MATRIX Framework

## Example 6: Regulatory Challenge

**Question:**  
Build a 3-year strategy for Uber to navigate autonomous vehicle regulations and prepare for self-driving future

**Context:**  
Uber needs to prepare for a world where autonomous vehicles (AVs) replace human drivers.

**Current state:**
- Revenue: $37B (2025)
- Rides: 10B rides/year globally
- Driver base: 6M drivers worldwide
- Current model: Marketplace (take rate 20-30% from drivers)

**The constraint:**
Waymo, Cruise (GM), and Tesla are deploying autonomous taxis. Uber's business model depends on human drivers — if AVs become mainstream, Uber becomes a logistics company competing with car manufacturers. Need to transition without alienating current drivers.

**Competitive landscape:**
- Waymo: 700K+ paid AV rides in Phoenix/SF, expanding to LA/Austin
- Cruise: Paused after incidents, but GM investing $8B in AV
- Tesla: Robotaxi network planned for 2026 launch

**Strategic opportunity:**
Uber has the demand (10B rides/year) and logistics network. Can partner with AV companies (already partnered with Waymo), build AV fleet management software, and own the customer relationship even if they don't own the cars. Drivers transition to fleet managers/cleaners/supervisors.

**Time Limit:** 25 minutes

**Recommended Framework:** MATRIX Framework
