# Goals & Metrics Example Questions

## Example 1: Social Media Feature

**Question:**  
Define success metrics for Instagram Reels

**Product Description:**
Instagram Reels is Meta's short-form video feature launched in 2020 to compete with TikTok. Users create 15-90 second videos with music, effects, and AR filters.

**Current state:**
- 2B Instagram users, 200M create Reels monthly
- Reels makes up 20% of time spent on Instagram (2024)
- Reels ads launched, but monetization lags TikTok

**Company Context:**
- Mission: Meta's mission is to give people the power to build community and bring the world closer together
- Strategic priority: Win short-form video before TikTok dominates Gen Z entirely
- Competitive landscape: TikTok (1.5B users, 52 min/day), YouTube Shorts (2B users, growing fast)

**The Challenge:**
Reels is growing but needs better creator monetization to retain top talent. TikTok pays creators directly (Creator Fund), Instagram only has brand deals. Need metrics framework that balances viewer engagement AND creator economics.

**Ecosystem:**
- Supply: Creators post Reels
- Demand: Viewers watch Reels
- Platform: Meta serves ads, takes revenue share

**Time Limit:** 25 minutes

**Recommended Framework:** G.A.M.E Framework

## Example 2: Marketplace Feature

**Question:**  
Define success metrics for Airbnb Experiences

**Product Description:**
Airbnb Experiences are activities hosted by locals (cooking classes, guided tours, workshops). Launched 2016 to expand beyond accommodation into full travel platform.

**Current state:**
- 40K Experiences in 1,000+ cities
- Experiences are <5% of Airbnb revenue ($8.4B total in 2023)
- Hosts earn avg $150/experience

**Company Context:**
- Mission: Create a world where anyone can belong anywhere
- Strategic priority: Diversify beyond accommodation (regulatory risk, competition)
- Competitive landscape: Viator (TripAdvisor), GetYourGuide, local tour operators

**The Challenge:**
Experiences has supply (hosts) but low demand (guest discovery is poor). Only 10% of Airbnb guests book an Experience. Need metrics to drive both supply quality AND demand growth.

**Ecosystem:**
- Supply: Experience hosts create listings
- Demand: Guests book and attend Experiences
- Platform: Airbnb takes 20% commission

**Time Limit:** 20 minutes

**Recommended Framework:** G.A.M.E Framework

## Example 3: Creator Monetization

**Question:**  
Define success metrics for YouTube Super Thanks (tipping feature for creators)

**Product Description:**
YouTube Super Thanks lets viewers tip creators $2-$50 during or after watching a video. Creator gets a highlighted comment, viewers support creators directly. YouTube takes 30% cut.

**Current state:**
- 2B YouTube users, 51M channels
- Super Thanks launched 2021, but <5% of eligible creators use it
- Patreon is dominant ($300M+ paid to creators annually)

**Company Context:**
- Mission: Give everyone a voice and show them the world
- Strategic priority: Keep top creators on YouTube (TikTok/Instagram competing for talent)
- Competitive landscape: Twitch (bits/subscriptions), Patreon (memberships), TikTok (gifts)

**The Challenge:**
Creators don't promote Super Thanks (30% cut too high vs Patreon's 5-12%). Viewers don't know it exists. Need metrics to drive both creator adoption AND viewer tipping behavior.

**Ecosystem:**
- Supply: Creators enable Super Thanks, encourage viewers to tip
- Demand: Viewers watch content, decide to tip
- Platform: YouTube takes 30% commission

**Time Limit:** 20 minutes

**Recommended Framework:** G.A.M.E Framework

**Framework Location:** `/plugins/goals-metrics/framework.md`

## Example 4: B2B Product

**Question:**  
Define success metrics for Slack Huddles (audio/video feature)

**Product Description:**
Slack Huddles are spontaneous audio/video calls launched 2021. Click to start instant conversation with channel members, screen share optional. Competing with Zoom, Teams for "quick sync" use case.

**Current state:**
- 18M Slack DAU, 750K paid customers
- Huddles used by 30% of teams, avg 3 huddles/week/team
- Zoom still dominates for scheduled meetings

**Company Context:**
- Mission: Make work life simpler, more pleasant, and more productive
- Strategic priority: Increase stickiness (prevent Teams from winning enterprise)
- Competitive landscape: Zoom (dominant), Microsoft Teams (bundled free), Google Meet

**The Challenge:**
Huddles launched but adoption plateaued. Teams use it for emergencies, not daily collaboration. Need metrics to drive habitual use (not just feature awareness).

**Ecosystem:**
- Users: Team members who start/join Huddles
- Admins: IT leaders who enable/disable features
- Platform: Slack provides infrastructure, monetizes via paid plans

**Time Limit:** 20 minutes

**Recommended Framework:** G.A.M.E Framework

## Example 5: Emerging Category

**Question:**  
Define success metrics for Snapchat Lens Studio (AR creation platform)

**Product Description:**
Lens Studio lets creators build custom AR filters (face lenses, world lenses). Launched 2017, now 300K+ lens creators. Lenses drive Snapchat engagement (lenses used in 75% of snaps).

**Current state:**
- 750M Snapchat users, 6B snaps/day
- 300K Lens creators, 3M+ lenses created

**Company Context:**
- Mission: Empower people to express themselves

- Competitive landscape: Instagram Effects, TikTok Effects, Unity (3D platform)

**The Challenge:**
Most lenses get <1K views (power law distribution). Creator monetization is zero (unlike TikTok Creator Fund). Need metrics to grow BOTH creator base AND lens usage.

**Ecosystem:**
- Supply: Lens creators build AR filters
- Demand: Snapchat users discover and use lenses
- Platform: Snapchat hosts lenses, drives discovery

## Example 6: Platform Expansion

**Question:**  
Define success metrics for Amazon Subscribe & Save (recurring delivery program)

**Product Description:**
Subscribe & Save offers automatic recurring delivery (monthly, bi-monthly) for household items with 5-15% discount. Launched 2007, competes with Target, Walmart subscription programs.

**Current state:**
- 200M Prime members, 30M use Subscribe & Save
- Avg 5 items per subscriber, $50/month revenue
- Subscribe & Save is 3% of Amazon retail revenue

**Company Context:**
- Mission: Be Earth's most customer-centric company
- Strategic priority: Lock in recurring revenue, compete with Walmart+
- Competitive landscape: Target Circle (RedCard), Walmart+ (delivery subscription)

**The Challenge:**
Churn is 40% annual (people forget to cancel, get annoyed). Need metrics that drive retention AND increase basket size without feeling predatory.

**Ecosystem:**
- Customers: Subscribe for convenience + discount
- Amazon: Wants recurring revenue, predictable demand
- Suppliers: Benefit from predictable orders

**Time Limit:** 20 minutes

**Recommended Framework:** G.A.M.E Framework

**Framework Location:** `/plugins/goals-metrics/framework.md`
