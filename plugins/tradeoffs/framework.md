# T.R.A.D.E Framework for Trade-off Questions

## What This Framework Is

T.R.A.D.E is a structured approach to evaluating competing objectives and making recommendations. It's used for "Should we prioritize X or Y?" questions.

**T.R.A.D.E stands for:**
- **T**ype of Trade-off & Context
- **R**eframe with Metrics
- **A**nalyze Dimensions
- **D**eep Dive & Decision Framework
- **E**xperiment & Evaluate

---

## When to Use This Framework

**Use for questions like:**
- "Should we prioritize [X] or [Y]?"
- "Trade-off between [A] and [B] — what do you recommend?"
- "How would you balance [competing objectives]?"
- "If you had to choose between [X] and [Y], which would you pick?"

**Core skill being tested:**
Can you think multi-dimensionally? Do you quantify trade-offs? Can you make a defensible recommendation? Do you acknowledge uncertainty and plan experiments?

---

## What This Framework Tests

### Strategic Thinking
- Do you understand the type of trade-off (short vs long term, user segments, stakeholders)?
- Do you connect to business strategy and product phase?
- Do you think about competitive dynamics?

### Analytical Rigor
- Do you quantify both sides with metrics?
- Do you evaluate multiple dimensions (not just one)?
- Do you score options systematically?

### Decision-Making
- Can you make a clear recommendation with rationale?
- Do you acknowledge trade-offs explicitly?
- Do you suggest alternatives or hybrid approaches?

### Execution Mindset
- Do you design experiments to validate decisions?
- Do you plan for different outcomes?
- Do you show what you'd do with results?

## The 5 Steps (15-20 minutes total)

### Step 1: Type of Trade-off & Context (3 minutes)

**What to do:**
Clarify what kind of trade-off this is and why it matters.

**Trade-off Categories:**
1. **Short vs Long term**: Revenue now vs user growth later
2. **Different User Segments**: Power users vs casual users
3. **Different Stakeholders**: Users vs advertisers vs shareholders
4. **Different Product Attributes**: Feature richness vs performance, Quality vs quantity
5. **Business Objectives**: Growth vs monetization vs engagement

**Structure:**
```
"Let me clarify the trade-off:

Type: [Category from above]

Competing objectives:
- Option X prioritizes: [What it optimizes for]
- Option Y prioritizes: [What it optimizes for]

Business context:
- Company mission: [How does each option align?]
- Product phase: [Early growth / Scaling / Mature / Declining]
- Strategic priority: [What matters most right now?]
- Competitive landscape: [What are competitors doing?]

Why this matters:
[Stakes, urgency, impact]"
```

**Example:**
```
"Let me clarify the trade-off:

Type: Short-term revenue vs long-term user growth

Competing objectives:
- Increase ad load: Prioritizes immediate revenue, risks user experience
- Reduce ad load: Prioritizes user retention, sacrifices short-term revenue

Business context:
- Company mission: Instagram wants to "bring the world closer together"
- Product phase: Mature product (2B users), but Reels is growth area
- Strategic priority: Fend off TikTok (user engagement critical)
- Competitive landscape: TikTok has lower ad load, YouTube Shorts just launched ads

Why this matters:
Reels is losing Gen Z to TikTok. If ad load is too high, users leave. If too low, we can't fund creator monetization (which also drives users away). This is an existential question."
```

**What evaluator looks for:**
- Named the trade-off type explicitly
- Stated what each option optimizes for
- Provided business context (mission, phase, strategy)
- Explained why this decision matters now

---

### Step 2: Reframe with Metrics (3 minutes)

**What to do:**
Quantify both sides of the trade-off.

**Structure:**
```
"Metrics framework:

Option X (e.g., Increase ad load):
- Primary benefit metric: [Metric] → Expected change: [+X%]
- Secondary benefit: [Metric] → [+Y%]
- Primary cost metric: [Metric] → Expected change: [-Z%]
- Secondary cost: [Metric] → [-W%]

Option Y (e.g., Reduce ad load):
- Primary benefit metric: [Metric] → [+A%]
- Secondary benefit: [Metric] → [+B%]
- Primary cost metric: [Metric] → [-C%]
- Secondary cost: [Metric] → [-D%]

Impact correlation:
[How changing one metric affects others]"
```

**Example:**
```
"Metrics framework:

Option A: Increase ad load (4 ads/hour → 6 ads/hour in Reels):
- Primary benefit: Ad revenue → +50% ($500M → $750M/year)
- Secondary benefit: Creator fund financing → +30% ($50M → $65M)
- Primary cost: User session time → -10% (15 min → 13.5 min/day)
- Secondary cost: Reels completion rate → -15% (65% → 55%)

Option B: Reduce ad load (4 ads/hour → 2 ads/hour):
- Primary benefit: User session time → +8% (15 min → 16.2 min/day)
- Secondary benefit: Reels completion rate → +12% (65% → 73%)
- Primary cost: Ad revenue → -40% ($500M → $300M/year)
- Secondary cost: Creator fund → -40% ($50M → $30M)

Impact correlation:
- Session time ↑ → Ad impressions ↑ (partially offsets ad load decrease)
- Completion rate ↑ → More content consumption → More creator posts
- Creator fund ↓ → Top creators leave → Content quality ↓ → User churn ↑"
```

**What evaluator looks for:**
- Quantified benefits AND costs for both options
- Used specific metrics (not vague "user satisfaction")
- Stated expected percentage changes
- Identified correlation between metrics

### Step 3: Analyze Dimensions (4 minutes)

**What to do:**
Evaluate the trade-off across multiple dimensions.

**Dimensions to Consider:**
1. **Magnitude**: How big is the gain/loss?
2. **Reversibility**: Can we undo this decision?
3. **Time Horizon**: When do we see impact? Short vs long term?
4. **Strategic Alignment**: Which aligns better with company goals?
5. **User Impact**: Which users affected, how severely?
6. **Competitive Dynamics**: What are competitors doing?

**Structure:**
```
"Multi-dimensional analysis:

1. Magnitude:
   - Option X: [Benefit size] vs [Cost size]
   - Option Y: [Benefit size] vs [Cost size]
   - Winner: [Which has better benefit/cost ratio]

2. Reversibility:
   - Option X: [Easy/Hard to reverse, why]
   - Option Y: [Easy/Hard to reverse, why]

3. Time Horizon:
   - Option X: [Short-term gain/Long-term cost OR reverse]
   - Option Y: [Short-term cost/Long-term gain OR reverse]

4. Strategic Alignment:
   - Mission: [Which aligns better]
   - Current priority: [Which supports strategic goal]

5. User Impact:
   - Option X: [Which users hurt/helped]
   - Option Y: [Which users hurt/helped]

6. Competitive:
   - Competitors: [What are they doing]
   - Differentiation: [Which maintains/loses competitive edge]

Summary: [Overall pattern]"
```

**Example:**
```
"Multi-dimensional analysis:

1. Magnitude:
   - Increase ad load: +$250M revenue, -1.5 min session time
   - Reduce ad load: +1.2 min session time, -$200M revenue
   - Winner: Revenue gain is larger in absolute terms

2. Reversibility:
   - Increase: HARD to reverse (users won't tolerate ad increases)
   - Reduce: EASY to reverse (can increase ads again if needed)

3. Time Horizon:
   - Increase: Short-term revenue gain, long-term user churn risk
   - Reduce: Short-term revenue hit, long-term engagement gain

4. Strategic Alignment:
   - Mission: Reduce ad load aligns better ("bring world closer together" = prioritize connection)
   - Current priority: Beating TikTok = need user engagement = reduce ad load

5. User Impact:
   - Increase: Hurts Gen Z most (low tolerance for ads), helps creators (more monetization)
   - Reduce: Helps Gen Z (better experience), hurts creators initially (less fund money)

6. Competitive:
   - TikTok: 2-3 ads/hour (lower than Instagram's 4)
   - YouTube Shorts: Just launched ads, testing load
   - Differentiation: Lower ad load helps us compete with TikTok

Summary: Reducing ad load is better for long-term strategy (beat TikTok) despite short-term revenue hit. Increasing ads is tempting but irreversible and strategically misaligned."
```

**What evaluator looks for:**
- Evaluated 5+ dimensions
- Compared options side-by-side
- Identified which option wins on each dimension
- Synthesized into overall pattern

### Step 4: Deep Dive & Decision Framework (5 minutes)

**What to do:**
Score options systematically and make a recommendation.

**Structure:**
```
"Decision framework:

Scoring criteria (weight each 0-10):

| Criterion | Weight | Option X Score | Weighted | Option Y Score | Weighted |
|-----------|--------|----------------|----------|----------------|----------|
| Revenue Impact | 20% | [0-10] | [X] | [0-10] | [Y] |
| User Retention | 25% | [0-10] | [X] | [0-10] | [Y] |
| User Experience | 20% | [0-10] | [X] | [0-10] | [Y] |
| Strategic Fit | 20% | [0-10] | [X] | [0-10] | [Y] |
| Competitive Position | 15% | [0-10] | [X] | [0-10] | [Y] |
| **Total** | 100% | | **[X]** | | **[Y]** |

ROI Calculation:
- Option X: [Benefit / Cost ratio]
- Option Y: [Benefit / Cost ratio]

Alternative approaches:
- [Hybrid option 1]
- [Hybrid option 2]

**RECOMMENDATION: [Option]**

Rationale:
- [Reason 1 with data]
- [Reason 2 with data]
- [Reason 3 with data]

Alternative path:
[If conditions change, what would make you switch]

Trade-offs acknowledged:
[What we're giving up with this choice]"
```

**Example:**
```
"Decision framework:

Scoring criteria:

| Criterion | Weight | Increase Ads | Weighted | Reduce Ads | Weighted |
|-----------|--------|--------------|----------|------------|----------|
| Revenue Impact | 20% | 9 | 1.8 | 3 | 0.6 |
| User Retention | 25% | 3 | 0.75 | 9 | 2.25 |
| User Experience | 20% | 2 | 0.4 | 9 | 1.8 |
| Strategic Fit | 20% | 4 | 0.8 | 9 | 1.8 |
| Competitive Position | 15% | 3 | 0.45 | 8 | 1.2 |
| **Total** | 100% | | **4.2** | | **7.65** |

ROI Calculation:
- Increase ads: $250M gain / 10% session time loss = High revenue ROI, low user ROI
- Reduce ads: 8% session time gain / $200M cost = High user ROI, low revenue ROI

Alternative approaches:
- **Dynamic ad load**: Show 2 ads/hour to Gen Z (high TikTok risk), 6 ads/hour to 35+ (low churn risk)
- **Value exchange**: Show ads but give creators 70% rev share (vs current 55%) to improve content quality

**RECOMMENDATION: Reduce ad load to 2 ads/hour**

Rationale:
1. Strategic alignment: Beating TikTok requires engagement, not short-term revenue. We're in an existential fight for Gen Z.
2. Reversibility: We can increase ads later. But if users leave for TikTok, they won't come back.
3. Creator dynamics: Short-term fund decrease hurts, but long-term (more engaged users → more views → more creator value) wins.

Alternative path:
If Gen Z churn rate doesn't improve after 2 quarters, revert to 4 ads/hour and invest in other retention levers (creation tools, social features).

Trade-offs acknowledged:
- $200M revenue loss hits earnings (need to communicate to investors)
- Creator fund decrease could cause top creator churn (mitigate with rev share increase)"
```

**What evaluator looks for:**
- Used scoring framework with weighted criteria
- Calculated scores for both options
- Made clear recommendation
- Provided data-driven rationale (not just intuition)
- Suggested alternative approaches
- Acknowledged trade-offs explicitly

### Step 5: Experiment & Evaluate (4 minutes)

**What to do:**
Design experiment to validate decision and plan for outcomes.

**Structure:**
```
"Experiment design:

Hypothesis:
[What we believe will happen]

Experiment setup:
- Treatment: [What we'll do]
- Control: [Baseline]
- Duration: [Time period]
- Sample size: [How many users]
- Randomization: [How we split]

Metrics to track:
- Primary: [Key metric]
- Secondary: [Supporting metrics]
- Guardrails: [What we can't break]

Decision criteria:

If results show [Outcome A]:
→ Action: [What we'll do]
→ Rationale: [Why]

If results show [Outcome B]:
→ Action: [What we'll do]
→ Rationale: [Why]

If results are inconclusive:
→ Action: [What we'll do]
→ Rationale: [Why]"
```

**Example:**
```
"Experiment design:

Hypothesis:
Reducing ad load from 4 → 2 ads/hour will increase Reels session time by 8%+ without hurting total ad revenue (due to higher engagement).

Experiment setup:
- Treatment: 2 ads/hour in Reels for 50% of users
- Control: 4 ads/hour (current) for 50% of users
- Duration: 8 weeks (2 full months to see retention effects)
- Sample size: 100M users (50M per group)
- Randomization: By user_id hash (stable assignment)

Metrics to track:
- Primary: Reels session time (minutes/day)
- Secondary: Reels DAU, completion rate, D7/D30 retention, ad revenue per user
- Guardrails: Total Instagram revenue (can't drop >5%), creator churn rate

Decision criteria:

If session time +8%+ AND revenue neutral or positive:
→ Action: Ship to 100%
→ Rationale: Clear win, both engagement and revenue

If session time +5-8% BUT revenue -10%:
→ Action: Ship to Gen Z only (18-24), keep 4 ads/hour for 25+
→ Rationale: Gen Z is strategic priority, older users tolerate ads better

If session time +3% AND revenue -15%:
→ Action: Don't ship, try dynamic ad load instead
→ Rationale: Gain too small for revenue cost

If results are inconclusive (session time +2%, revenue flat):
→ Action: Extend experiment to 12 weeks, segment by user cohort
→ Rationale: Need more time to see retention effects, heavy vs light users may differ"
```

**What evaluator looks for:**
- Defined clear hypothesis
- Designed proper A/B test (treatment, control, duration)
- Specified metrics (primary, secondary, guardrails)
- Planned for 3 outcomes (positive, negative, inconclusive)
- Showed decision tree based on data

## Evaluation Rubric (Total: 10 points)

### Component 1: Type & Context (2 points)

**2 points (Excellent):**
- Named trade-off type explicitly
- Stated what each option optimizes for
- Provided business context (mission, phase, competitive landscape)
- Explained why decision matters now

**1 point (Partial):**
- Identified trade-off but weak context OR
- No strategic framing

**0 points (Missing):**
- Jumped straight to recommendation without framing

---

### Component 2: Reframe with Metrics (2 points)

**2 points (Excellent):**
- Quantified benefits AND costs for both options
- Used specific metrics with percentage changes
- Identified metric correlations

**1 point (Partial):**
- Quantified one side but not both OR
- Vague metrics ("user satisfaction" without definition)

**0 points (Missing):**
- No quantification

---

### Component 3: Analyze Dimensions (2 points)

**2 points (Excellent):**
- Evaluated 5+ dimensions (magnitude, reversibility, time, strategy, users, competitive)
- Compared options side-by-side
- Synthesized overall pattern

**1 point (Partial):**
- Evaluated 2-3 dimensions OR
- No synthesis

**0 points (Missing):**
- One-dimensional analysis

---

### Component 4: Decision Framework (2 points)

**2 points (Excellent):**
- Used scoring framework
- Made clear recommendation with data-driven rationale
- Suggested alternatives
- Acknowledged trade-offs explicitly

**1 point (Partial):**
- Recommendation but weak rationale OR
- No alternatives OR
- Didn't acknowledge trade-offs

**0 points (Missing):**
- No recommendation

---

### Component 5: Experiment & Evaluate (2 points)

**2 points (Excellent):**
- Designed proper A/B test
- Specified metrics and decision criteria
- Planned for 3 outcomes (positive, negative, inconclusive)

**1 point (Partial):**
- Mentioned experiment but incomplete design OR
- No decision criteria

**0 points (Missing):**
- No experiment plan

---

## Scoring Interpretation

**9-10 points:** Excellent. Hire strong. Multi-dimensional thinking, clear decision framework.
**7-8 points:** Strong. Likely hire. Good analysis, minor gaps (e.g., weak on experiments).
**5-6 points:** Decent. Mixed signals. Made recommendation but weak multi-dimensional analysis.
**3-4 points:** Weak. Likely no hire. One-dimensional or no clear recommendation.
**0-2 points:** Poor. No hire. Couldn't structure the trade-off.

## Time Management Tips

**Total time: 15-20 minutes**

- **Type & Context**: 3 min
- **Reframe with Metrics**: 3 min
- **Analyze Dimensions**: 4 min
- **Decision Framework**: 5 min (most important)
- **Experiment**: 4 min

**If running out of time:**
- Compress Type & Context (2 min)
- Keep Decision Framework (this is the core)
- Quick experiment plan (2 min)

## Common Failure Modes

### Failure Mode 1: Picked a Side Immediately
Said "Obviously we should do X" without analyzing trade-offs.

**Feedback:** "Trade-off questions test your ability to think multi-dimensionally. Use T.R.A.D.E to evaluate systematically before recommending."

---

### Failure Mode 2: No Quantification
Said "X is better for users, Y is better for revenue" without metrics.

**Feedback:** "Always quantify. 'Better for users' is vague. By how much? What specific metrics? With numbers, you can compare apples to apples."

---

### Failure Mode 3: One-Dimensional Analysis
Only considered revenue, ignored user impact, competitive dynamics, reversibility.

**Feedback:** "Analyze multiple dimensions: magnitude, time horizon, reversibility, strategic fit, user impact, competitive position. Trade-offs are multi-faceted."

---

### Failure Mode 4: No Clear Recommendation
Analyzed well but said "It depends" without picking a side.

**Feedback:** "PMs must make decisions under uncertainty. Pick the best option based on available data, state your rationale, acknowledge trade-offs."

---

### Failure Mode 5: Ignored Alternatives
Presented as binary choice without considering hybrid approaches.

**Feedback:** "Trade-offs are rarely black/white. Consider: Dynamic approach? Segment by user type? Phased rollout? Hybrid solutions often win."

---

### Failure Mode 6: No Experiment Plan
Made recommendation without validating approach.

**Feedback:** "Always design experiments. Even strong recommendations need validation. Define hypothesis, metrics, decision criteria."
