# Trade-off Example Questions

## Example 1: Ad Load vs User Experience

**Question:**  
Should Instagram Reels prioritize increasing ad load (for revenue) or reducing ad load (for user experience)?

**The Trade-off:**

**Option A: Increase ad load**
- What it is: Increase from 4 ads/hour → 6 ads/hour in Reels
- Key benefit: +50% ad revenue ($500M → $750M/year), fund creator monetization
- Key cost: User session time likely drops 10-15%, potential Gen Z churn

**Option B: Reduce ad load**
- What it is: Decrease from 4 ads/hour → 2 ads/hour in Reels
- Key benefit: User session time likely increases 8-10%, better TikTok competition
- Key cost: Ad revenue drops 40% ($500M → $300M/year), less creator funding

**Why they're in tension:**
Reels is losing Gen Z to TikTok (2-3 ads/hour). Higher ad load funds creators but hurts UX. Lower ad load improves engagement but reduces revenue and creator earnings.

**Product Context:**
Instagram Reels is Meta's short-form video feature. 2B Instagram users, 200M create Reels monthly, Reels is 20% of Instagram time. TikTok has 50% higher daily time among 18-24.

Current challenge: Need to win Gen Z back from TikTok.

**Business Context:**
- Mission: Bring the world closer together
- Strategic priority: Beat TikTok before they dominate Gen Z
- Competitive landscape: TikTok 1.5B users (52 min/day), YouTube Shorts 2B users
- Product phase: Scaling (launched 2020, now mature feature)

**Time Limit:** 20 minutes

## Example 2: Driver Pay vs Rider Prices

**Question:**  
Should Uber prioritize increasing driver pay (for supply) or keeping rider prices low (for demand)?

**The Trade-off:**

**Option A: Increase driver pay**
- What it is: Raise driver commission from 75% → 85% of fare
- Key benefit: More drivers online (+20% supply), shorter wait times, better service quality
- Key cost: Either raise rider prices (+15%) OR reduce Uber's take rate (margin compression)

**Option B: Keep prices low**
- What it is: Maintain current 75% driver commission, optimize prices for demand
- Key benefit: Maximize rider growth, maintain competitive pricing vs Lyft
- Key cost: Driver churn (+10%), longer wait times, service quality decline

**Why they're in tension:**
Uber is a two-sided marketplace. Drivers want higher pay (inflation, gas prices). Riders want low prices (recession, budget consciousness). Both are necessary for flywheel.

**Product Context:**
Uber has 130M riders, 5M drivers globally. Take rate is 25% (driver gets 75%). Lyft offers 78% to drivers. Driver churn is 40%/year, rider churn is 15%/year.

Current challenge: Post-pandemic driver shortage + inflation → supply constrained in many cities.

**Business Context:**
- Mission: We ignite opportunity by setting the world in motion
- Strategic priority: Return to profitability (just turned profitable Q1 2024)
- Competitive landscape: Lyft (18% market share), local competitors in APAC
- Product phase: Mature (launched 2009, market leader)

**Time Limit:** 20 minutes

**Recommended Framework:** T.R.A.D.E Framework

**Framework Location:** `/plugins/tradeoffs/framework.md`

## Example 3: Content Quality vs Quantity

**Question:**  
Should YouTube prioritize high-quality professional content or high-volume creator content?

**The Trade-off:**

**Option A: Professional content**
- What it is: Invest $10B in premium shows/movies (Netflix competitor), promote in recommendations
- Key benefit: Higher watch time per video (+40%), premium ad inventory, brand safety
- Key cost: Less creator content discovered, alienate long-tail creators, production costs

**Option B: Creator content**
- What it is: Double creator fund ($5B → $10B/year), improve discovery for small creators
- Key benefit: More diverse content, community loyalty, sustainable content pipeline
- Key cost: Lower watch time per video, less premium ad inventory

**Why they're in tension:**
Professional content has higher watch time but costs $10M+/hour to produce. Creator content is free (creators produce it) but lower engagement per video. YouTube needs both but algorithm must pick priority.

**Product Context:**
YouTube has 2.5B users, 51M creators. Premium content (YouTube Originals) gets 5M views/episode. Creator content gets 100B+ daily views but <10K views per median video.

Current challenge: Netflix/Disney+ competing for premium eyeballs. TikTok competing for creator talent.

**Business Context:**
- Mission: Give everyone a voice and show them the world
- Strategic priority: Maintain dominance in video before streaming services fragment market
- Competitive landscape: Netflix (250M subs), TikTok (creator exodus)
- Product phase: Mature but facing disruption

**Time Limit:** 20 minutes

**Recommended Framework:** T.R.A.D.E Framework

## Example 4: Feature Richness vs Simplicity

**Question:**  
Should Notion prioritize adding more features (power users) or simplifying (new users)?

**The Trade-off:**

**Option A: Add features**
- What it is: Ship advanced features (databases, formulas, API integrations, 50+ new features/year)
- Key benefit: Power users stay (low churn), enterprise adoption, higher ARPU
- Key cost: New user onboarding failure rate increases, "too complex" perception

**Option B: Simplify**
- What it is: Pause features, simplify UI, improve onboarding, reduce feature set
- Key benefit: New user activation improves (+30%), faster growth, better virality
- Key cost: Power users churn to Coda/Airtable, enterprise deals slow, ARPU drops

**Why they're in tension:**
Notion's power comes from flexibility (all-in-one workspace). But flexibility = complexity. New users get overwhelmed. Power users want MORE features. Can't please both.

**Product Context:**
Notion has 30M users, 80% free tier, 20% paid. New user activation is 40% (industry avg: 60%). Power user retention is 95% (excellent). Enterprise segment growing 200%/year.

Current challenge: Growth slowing (from 100% YoY → 40% YoY) due to activation issues.

**Business Context:**
- Mission: Make toolmaking ubiquitous
- Strategic priority: Reach 100M users (need better activation)
- Competitive landscape: Confluence (enterprise), Coda (power users), Google Docs (simplicity)
- Product phase: Scaling (Series C, $10B valuation)

**Time Limit:** 15 minutes

**Recommended Framework:** T.R.A.D.E Framework
